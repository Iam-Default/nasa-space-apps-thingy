JWST Structure

The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) is a groundbreaking infrared observatory featuring a 6.5-meter primary mirror made of 18 hexagonal beryllium segments coated with gold. This design optimizes light-gathering capacity. Its sunshield, the size of a tennis court, blocks heat from the Sun, Earth, and Moon to maintain near-absolute zero temperatures. JWST includes four main instruments: NIRCam for imaging, NIRSpec for spectroscopy, MIRI for cooler celestial objects, and FGS/NIRISS for precision guidance. It operates at the second Lagrange point (L2), ensuring an unobstructed view of the cosmos.
