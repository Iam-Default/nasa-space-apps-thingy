JWST Journey

The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) has four primary scientific objectives: 1. **Studying the Early Universe:** JWST aims to observe the formation of the first stars and galaxies post-Big Bang using its infrared capabilities to detect faint, redshifted light. 2. **Understanding Galaxy Formation:** It will explore how galaxies evolve and interact with dark matter and black holes. 3. **Star and Planet Formation:** JWST will study protostars and protoplanetary disks to understand star and planet formation. 4. **Searching for Habitable Worlds:** It will analyze exoplanet atmospheres for signs of life using transmission spectroscopy, potentially reshaping our knowledge of the universe.
