JWST Creation

The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) has a lengthy development history, originating in the early 1990s as a successor to the Hubble Space Telescope (HST). Recognizing Hubble's limitations in infrared observations, NASA initiated the Next Generation Space Telescope (NGST) project to explore the early universe over 13 billion years old. Renamed in 2002 in honor of NASA administrator James E. Webb, JWST faced numerous technical, financial, and managerial challenges. Initial budgets were around $500 million, but costs ballooned to over $10 billion due to design complexities and delays. Key milestones included the successful installation of the mirror in 2016 and rigorous testing through the late 2010s. After being transported to French Guiana, JWST launched on December 25, 2021. Its history reflects the dedication and innovation of the global scientific community, marking it as a pivotal instrument for 21st-century space exploration.
