James Webb Space Telescope

The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) is the most advanced space telescope, designed to enhance our understanding of the universe. A collaboration among NASA, ESA, and CSA, it was launched on December 25, 2021, and operates in infrared wavelengths, allowing it to observe hidden cosmic phenomena. Its 6.5-meter primary mirror, significantly larger than Hubble's, enhances its light-collecting ability. JWST aims to explore the origins of the universe, star formation, and potential extraterrestrial life. Together with Hubble, it will provide a comprehensive view of cosmic evolution, promising groundbreaking discoveries over its decade-long mission.
